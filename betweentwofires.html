Between Two Fires

By Christopher Buehlman

Alistair Konrad

/

Nov 11th, 2022

Between Two Fires is a historical fiction novel by Christopher Buehlman that takes place in the midst of the Great Fire of London in 1666. The story follows a young woman named Honor Bright, who has recently fled her home in rural England to escape a forced marriage. As she struggles to survive in the crowded and chaotic city, she finds herself caught between two men: one a wealthy merchant, the other a charismatic actor.

One of the standout aspects of Between Two Fires is the vivid and immersive depiction of London during the Great Fire. Buehlman brings the city to life with rich detail and historical accuracy, and the fire itself is a formidable presence that drives the plot forward. The characters are well-developed and likable, and the relationships between them are complex and believable.

"Buehlman brings the city to life with rich detail and historical accuracy..."

Overall, Between Two Fires is a well-written and engaging historical fiction novel that transports readers back to a time of great upheaval and danger. The vivid depiction of London during the Great Fire and the well-developed characters make it a must-read for fans of the genre.
