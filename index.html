Notes from Underground

By Fyodor Dostoevsky

Notes from Underground is a novella by Fyodor Dostoevsky that was first published in 1864. It is considered to be one of the first works of existentialist literature and explores themes of isolation, alienation, and the human condition. The story is told through the eyes of the protagonist, a bitter and cynical man known only as the Underground Man. He is a misanthrope who rails against society and its norms, and the novella is a series of rants and musings in which he reflects on his own disillusionment and resentment towards the world around him. One of the standout aspects of Notes from Underground is the way it captures the inner turmoil of its protagonist. The Underground Man is a complex and deeply flawed character, and his thoughts and feelings are vividly and poignantly portrayed. Dostoevsky's writing is sharp and incisive, and the novella is a powerful and thought-provoking exploration of the human psyche.

Between Two Fires

By Christopher Buehlman

Between Two Fires is a historical fiction novel by Christopher Buehlman that takes place in the midst of the Great Fire of London in 1666. The story follows a young woman named Honor Bright, who has recently fled her home in rural England to escape a forced marriage. As she struggles to survive in the crowded and chaotic city, she finds herself caught between two men: one a wealthy merchant, the other a charismatic actor. One of the standout aspects of Between Two Fires is the vivid and immersive depiction of London during the Great Fire. Buehlman brings the city to life with rich detail and historical accuracy, and the fire itself is a formidable presence that drives the plot forward. The characters are well-developed and likable, and the relationships between them are complex and believable.
