Notes From Underground

By Fyodor Dostoevsky

Alistair Konrad

/

Nov 11th, 2022

Notes from Underground is a novella by Fyodor Dostoevsky that was first published in 1864. It is considered to be one of the first works of existentialist literature and explores themes of isolation, alienation, and the human condition.

"[Individuality], freedom, and the consequences of technological advancement."

The story is told through the eyes of the protagonist, a bitter and cynical man known only as the Underground Man. He is a misanthrope who rails against society and its norms, and the novella is a series of rants and musings in which he reflects on his own disillusionment and resentment towards the world around him.

One of the standout aspects of Notes from Underground is the way it captures the inner turmoil of its protagonist. The Underground Man is a complex and deeply flawed character, and his thoughts and feelings are vividly and poignantly portrayed. Dostoevsky's writing is sharp and incisive, and the novella is a powerful and thought-provoking exploration of the human psyche.

"Huxley's writing is sharp, incisive, and thought-provoking..."

Overall, Notes from Underground is a must-read for fans of existentialist literature or anyone interested in the inner workings of the human mind. Its themes and ideas are timeless and still relevant today, and it is a powerful and thought-provoking work that will stay with you long after you've finished reading.
